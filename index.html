Here's why you should quit smoking as early as now!

Effects upon quiting.


  1. Within six hours your heart rate will slow and your blood pressure will become more stable.
  2. Within one day your bloodstream will be almost nicotine free, the level of carbon monoxide in your blood will have dropped, and oxygen will be reaching your heart and muscles more easily.
  3. Within one week your sense of taste and smell may have improved.
  4. Within six months your stress levels are likely to have dropped, and you are less likely to be coughing up phlegm.
  5. After one year your lungs will be healthier and breathing will be easier than if you’d kept smoking.
  6. Within two to five years your risk of heart disease will have dropped significantly (and will continue to do so over time).
  7. Within five years, a woman’s risk of cervical cancer will be the same as if she had never smoked.
  8. After 10 to 15 years your risk of lung cancer will be half that of someone of a similar age who keeps smoking.
  9. After 20 years your risk of heart attack and stroke will be similar to that of someone who has never smoked. Click here for more information.

Tips How To Quit


  1. List your reasons to quit.
  2. Tell people you're quitting.
  3. If you have tried to quit before, remember what worked.
  4. Use stop smoking aids.
  5. Have a plan if you are tempted to smoke.
  6. List your smoking triggers and how to avoid them.
  7. Keep cravings at bay by keeping busy.
  8. Exercise away the urge.
  9. Join Us for support and advice. Click here for more information.
